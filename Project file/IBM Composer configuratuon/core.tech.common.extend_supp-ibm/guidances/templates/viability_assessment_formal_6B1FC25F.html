Шаблон: Viability Assessment (formal)
This template can be used as a basis for any kind of viability assessment (e.g. formal or informal, part of a QA review or ad-hoc etc).

Основное описание

1. Approach
Describe the approach that was taken to performing the viability assessment together with any other background information. This includes items such as:

  • Date and location of the review
  • Context of the assessment (part of a QA, by invitation from the client etc)
  • Formal vs. informal
  • What individuals, groups, departments were interviewed
  • What phase the assessment was performed in
  • What documentation was provided for the assessment

2. General Findings and Recommendations
This section summarizes the assessment's major findings and recommendations.  Documentation should be brief and actionable, with responsibilities to carry forward recommendations clearly defined.  A table or a header/sub-header organization can be used to present this information. For example:

3. Risk Ranking Worksheet

A risk ranking worksheet is a means to prioritize risks during a viability assessment.  Not all risks are equally important.  In the General Findings and Recommendations section, the architect will want to focus upon the high risk/high payback items, and leave reporting lower risks to an appendix or supplemental reading.

Risks can be actioned in one of the following ways:

  • Remove - Risk can be removed from the project by changing the solution's characteristics.  For example, risk due to the use of an unproved development tool can be removed by substituting a proven tool.
  • Accept - Risks can be accepted without a change to the architecture.
  • Contain - Adding estimation contingency, mitigation tasks, excess capacity or other measures to reduce the probability of a risk occurring can contain risks.  For example, risk due to geographically distributed development sites can be mitigated by developing a workroom, conducting standing joint meetings, etc. The risk, though, remains an inherent characteristic of the proposed solution.


4. Architecture Checklist

Checklist or questionnaire should be created to structure and organize the viability assessment.  This is used to uncover the major items that will be documented in the General Recommendations and Findings section.  A suggested checklist is provided in the Validation and Verification section of this work product.


5. Recommendation for Next Review

Document the anticipated date for the next review so that participants and project management can plan accordingly.  The need for a follow-up review will depend upon the specification in the project's engagement template, the seriousness of the findings, and/or any external IBM QA policies.
